What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it? It creates an empty repository that you must add files to. If you dont add files, they files will be untracked and you will get an error.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers? The staging area is a middle ground between the repository and the working directory. It allows us the abilty to check what we are adding to a commit.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change? You can choose which change you wish to commit while keeping all the changes in the staging area.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help? When you are trying different approaches to solve a problem, the branches allow you to test your work without effecting other test cases.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?
It helps you organize your branches and see how each branch interacts with other branches and the master.

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do? It combines the two branches together into one branch. We represent it literally as a branch merging into another branch.
